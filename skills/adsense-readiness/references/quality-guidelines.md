# Content Quality Guidelines for AdSense

Best practices for creating high-quality sites that succeed with AdSense monetization.

## Core Principle

> "Focus on the users and all else will follow."

Building high-quality sites takes effort and time. Publishers who focus on users instead of quick, deceptive techniques experience long-term revenue growth and success.

---

## Content Quality Requirements

### Originality and Value

**Do:**
- Create original content that sets your site apart
- Provide substantial value compared to sites covering similar subjects
- Focus on topics you're knowledgeable and enthusiastic about
- Give users a reason to visit AND return

**Don't:**
- Duplicate content across multiple pages, subdomains, or sites
- Use doorway pages or doorway domains
- Create pages with little to no content
- Optimize pages solely for specific keywords/phrases

### Information-Rich Content

**Do:**
- Make content informative and well-organized
- Organize content for easy user navigation
- Focus on engaging the user with relevant information
- Provide interactive experiences based on user interests

**Don't:**
- Create overlapping or redundant content
- Generate content to trick bots (e.g., irrelevant high-paying keywords)
- Trick users into navigating through pages just to view ads

### Promise Delivery

**Do:**
- Deliver the information or service your site appears to offer
- Use keywords appropriately in context with content
- Enable users to easily find promised products, goods, or services

**Don't:**
- Trick users with bait-and-switch tactics
- Create sites that appear to offer products but only show ads
- Create sites perceived as untrustworthy

---

## Site Organization

### Navigation Structure

- Users should easily navigate through pages
- Users should quickly find information or services they seek
- Clear menu structure and internal linking
- Logical content hierarchy

### Ad-to-Content Balance

- Ads should be an additional resource for users
- Ads should enrich page value, not dominate it
- Ads should not be more prominent than content
- Ads should not overwhelm the user experience

## Pages Not Suited for Monetization

Even valuable sites have pages that shouldn't show ads:

### Exit/Thank You Pages
- Pages shown after purchases or form submissions
- "Thank you for your visit" messages
- Confirmation screens

### Error Pages
- 404 pages (content not found)
- 500 pages (server errors)
- Any error state pages

### Behavioral/Utility Pages
- Alert or notification screens
- Navigation-only pages
- Loading screens
- Interstitial pages without content

### Under Construction
- Pages not yet complete
- "Coming soon" placeholders
- Partial content pages

## Google Search Essentials

### Technical Requirements
- Meet minimum technical requirements for Google to show pages in search
- Most sites pass automatically

### Spam Policies
- Avoid behaviors leading to lower ranking or omission from search
- Focus on best content and user experience

### Key Best Practices

1. **Create helpful, reliable, people-first content**
2. **Use natural language** - Words people actually search for
3. **Place keywords in prominent locations** - Title, main heading, alt text, link text
4. **Make links crawlable** - Enable Google to find other pages
5. **Promote your site** - Be active in relevant communities
6. **Follow media-specific guidelines** - Images, videos, structured data, JavaScript
7. **Enable relevant search features** - Rich results, featured snippets
8. **Control content visibility** - Use appropriate methods for content you don't want indexed

## Quality Self-Assessment Questions

Ask yourself about each page:

1. Does this page provide substantial value vs. competitors?
2. Is the content original and not duplicated elsewhere on my site?
3. Is the site well-organized with clear navigation?
4. Does the content match what users expect from the page title/description?
5. Would a user bookmark this page or recommend it?
6. Is there more content than ads on this page?
7. Would I trust this site with my information?
8. Is this content created for users or for search engines?
9. Does this page have a clear purpose?
10. Would removing ads make this page worthless?

If the answer to #10 is "yes," the page likely shouldn't have ads.

## Long-Term Success Factors

- **Consistency** - Regular content updates
- **Expertise** - Demonstrable knowledge in your subject area
- **Trustworthiness** - Transparent about who you are
- **User engagement** - Comments, shares, return visits
- **Mobile-friendly** - Responsive design, fast loading
- **Accessibility** - Usable by people with disabilities
