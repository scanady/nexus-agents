# Diagnostic Questions

Question bank for gathering change initiative inputs when information is missing. Select questions based on what gaps exist in the user's provided context.

---

## Core Change Context Questions

Use when the basic change description is unclear or incomplete:

1. **What specifically is changing?** Describe the current state and the desired future state in concrete terms — what will people do differently on a daily basis?
2. **What is the business driver?** Why is this change happening now? What happens if you don't change? (competitive threat, regulatory deadline, cost pressure, growth opportunity, customer demand)
3. **How did this initiative originate?** Top-down mandate, bottom-up request, external catalyst, or strategic planning process?
4. **What is the timeline?** Is there a hard deadline (regulatory, contractual) or is the timeline flexible?
5. **Has this been attempted before?** What happened? What was learned? Why is this time different?

## Organization Context Questions

Use when you don't understand the organizational landscape:

6. **How large is the impacted population?** Number of people, teams, departments, locations, geographies.
7. **What is the organizational structure?** Hierarchical or flat? Centralized or distributed? Matrixed or functional?
8. **How does the organization work day-to-day?** Remote, hybrid, in-person? Formal or informal culture? Fast-moving or deliberate?
9. **What is the current change load?** How many other active change initiatives are running? Is there visible change fatigue?
10. **How does this organization typically handle change?** Describe the last significant change — how was it communicated, received, and adopted?

## Sponsorship and Leadership Questions

Use when sponsorship clarity is low:

11. **Who is the executive sponsor?** Name and title. How senior? How visible to the impacted population?
12. **How committed is the sponsor?** Will they show up consistently — town halls, floor walks, Q&A sessions — or are they a name on a memo?
13. **Is the leadership team aligned?** Are there known disagreements or competing priorities among the senior leadership regarding this change?
14. **Who are the key decision-makers?** Beyond the sponsor, who must approve milestones, budgets, and design decisions?
15. **Are there known opponents at the leadership level?** Anyone who publicly or privately opposes this change?

## Stakeholder and Impact Questions

Use when stakeholder landscape is unclear:

16. **Who are the primary impacted groups?** Which teams, roles, or departments will experience the most disruption to their daily work?
17. **Are there groups who benefit from the current state?** Who might lose status, autonomy, expertise, or comfort if this change succeeds?
18. **Are unions, works councils, or employee representatives involved?** Any formal consultation or negotiation requirements?
19. **Are external stakeholders affected?** Customers, partners, vendors, regulators?
20. **Who are the informal influencers?** Beyond formal hierarchy, who do people listen to and trust?

## Technology-Specific Questions

Use when the change involves technology adoption:

21. **What system or tool is being implemented or replaced?** Name, vendor, purpose.
22. **What is it replacing?** How long has the current system been in use? How attached are people to it?
23. **What is the technology proficiency baseline?** Are impacted users generally tech-savvy or tech-resistant?
24. **Will there be a parallel running period?** Will old and new systems run simultaneously?
25. **What integrations and dependencies exist?** Does this technology change affect other systems or processes?

## Process-Specific Questions

Use when the change involves process redesign:

26. **Which processes are changing?** List the specific workflows, procedures, or policies affected.
27. **Are the new processes designed yet?** Or is process design part of this initiative?
28. **Who owns the current processes?** Will ownership shift?
29. **How are exceptions handled today vs. in the new state?** Exception handling is where process changes break down.
30. **What compliance or audit requirements apply?** Any regulatory constraints on the process change?

## Culture and Mindset Questions

Use when the change involves cultural transformation:

31. **What specific behaviors are you trying to change?** Not values — observable behaviors in meetings, decisions, interactions.
32. **What does "success" look like in 12 months?** Describe the cultural shift in terms people would recognize walking through the office.
33. **What reinforcement mechanisms exist?** Do performance reviews, promotion criteria, and recognition programs currently reward the desired behaviors?
34. **Is leadership modeling the desired behaviors?** Culture change fails if leaders don't visibly embody it.
35. **How will you measure cultural shift?** Pulse surveys, behavioral observations, outcome metrics?

## Resource and Capacity Questions

Use when you need to understand execution constraints:

36. **What budget is allocated?** For communication, training, tools, external support.
37. **Is there a dedicated change management team?** Or is this being run off the side of someone's desk?
38. **What communication channels are available?** Intranet, email, Slack/Teams, town halls, digital signage, newsletters?
39. **Is there an existing training infrastructure?** LMS, classroom space, virtual training platform?
40. **What external support is available?** Consultants, vendors, implementation partners?

---

## Usage Guidelines

- **Ask only what you need.** Don't fire all 40 questions. Identify gaps in what the user provided and select 3-10 targeted questions.
- **Batch by theme.** Group related questions together rather than jumping between topics.
- **Explain why you're asking.** Each question should feel relevant, not bureaucratic. Frame questions in terms of what you're trying to design: "To build the communication plan, I need to understand..."
- **Accept partial answers.** If the user doesn't know, document the gap as an assumption and move forward.
- **Prioritize impact.** If you can only ask 3 questions, ask: What's changing? Who's most affected? What happened last time you tried to change something?
