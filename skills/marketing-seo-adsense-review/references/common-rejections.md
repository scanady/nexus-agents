# Common AdSense Rejections — Causes and Fixes

Complete reference of all common reasons Google rejects AdSense applications, ordered by frequency. Each includes what Google sees, why it's a problem, and specific steps to fix it.

---

## 1. Low Value Content (Most Common)

**What Google's email says:**
> "Your site does not yet meet the criteria of use in the Google publisher network."

**What it means:**
Google reviewed your entire site and determined it doesn't provide enough original, high-quality content to justify showing ads to users.

**Common triggers:**
- Too few pages with substantive content (< 15 pages)
- Short page content (< 300 words per page)
- Generic, templated, or AI-generated content
- No clear niche or topic focus
- Content that doesn't demonstrate expertise
- Site appears created for ads rather than for users

**How to fix:**
1. See `low-value-content-signals.md` for detailed guidance
2. Add at least 15-20 pages of 500+ word original content
3. Focus on a clear niche with demonstrated expertise
4. Remove placeholder or thin pages
5. Wait 2-4 weeks for Google to recrawl before resubmitting

**Relevant policies:**
- https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/10015918
- https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/7299563

---

## 2. Insufficient Content

**What Google's email says:**
> "Your site was found to have too little text, and/or your site was deemed to be 'under construction.'"

**What it means:**
A more severe version of "low value content" — the site literally doesn't have enough text content.

**Common triggers:**
- Site is primarily images or video with minimal text
- Pages have only headlines without body text
- Site still has "coming soon" or template pages
- Very few pages exist (< 5-10)
- Ad code placed on test/empty pages

**How to fix:**
1. Ensure every page has substantial text (300+ words minimum)
2. Content must be complete sentences and paragraphs, not just headlines
3. Remove all "coming soon" and placeholder content
4. Don't place ad code on test/empty pages
5. Build the site fully before applying

**Relevant policy:**
- https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/81904

---

## 3. Site Navigation Issues

**What Google's email says:**
> "During our review of your site, we found your site difficult to navigate."

**What it means:**
Google's reviewer couldn't easily browse your site. Navigation was confusing, broken, or missing.

**Common triggers:**
- No navigation bar or menu
- Broken nav links
- Pages behind login/paywall
- Excessive redirects
- Pop-ups blocking content
- Pages under construction in the navigation
- No consistent navigation across pages
- Links leading to missing pages (404s)

**How to fix:**
1. Add a clear, consistent navigation bar on every page
2. Link to all main sections from the navigation
3. Fix all broken links
4. Ensure no pages require login to view content
5. Remove excessive pop-ups or interstitials
6. Organize content with clear categories

**Relevant policy:**
- https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/48182

## 4. Content Policy Violations

**What Google's email says:**
> "Your site doesn't comply with AdSense policies."

**What it means:**
Your site contains content from one of Google's prohibited content categories.

**Common triggers:**
- Adult or sexual content (even partial)
- Violence or dangerous content
- Illegal activity or products
- Drug-related content
- Gambling content (in some jurisdictions)
- Hate speech or derogatory content
- Deceptive practices

**How to fix:**
1. Review ALL pages against Google Publisher Policies content categories
2. Remove any content that falls into prohibited categories
3. If content is borderline, add disclaimers and ensure it's handled responsibly
4. Check user-generated content (comments, forum posts) for violations too

**Relevant policy:**
- https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/10502938

## 5. Traffic Source Issues

**What Google's email says:**
> "Your site has issues with its traffic sources."

**What it means:**
Google detected that your site's traffic comes from prohibited or low-quality sources.

**Common triggers:**
- Paid-to-click traffic
- Pop-under traffic
- Auto-redirect traffic
- Bot traffic
- Email spam driving traffic
- Social media spam
- Participating in traffic exchange programs

**How to fix:**
1. Rely on organic traffic from search engines
2. Remove participation in traffic exchange programs
3. Don't buy traffic or use click services
4. Focus on SEO and legitimate social media promotion
5. Monitor Google Analytics for suspicious traffic patterns

**Relevant policy:**
- https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/48182

---

## 6. Duplicate/Scraped Content

**What Google's email says:**
Usually falls under "low value content" or "content quality" rejection.

**What it means:**
Your content is copied, scraped, or duplicated from other sources.

**Common triggers:**
- Content copied from other websites
- RSS feed aggregation without original commentary
- Product descriptions copied from manufacturers
- Article spinning tools
- Same content appearing on multiple pages of your site
- Syndicated content without added value

**How to fix:**
1. Remove or rewrite all copied content
2. Add original commentary, analysis, or perspective to any curated content
3. Consolidate duplicate pages
4. Create genuinely original content
5. Use canonical tags if there's a legitimate reason for similar pages

**Relevant policy:**
- https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/10015918

---

## 7. Unsupported Language

**What Google's email says:**
> "The majority of your site's content is in a language that AdSense doesn't currently support."

**What it means:**
Your site's primary language isn't supported by AdSense.

**How to fix:**
1. Check the list of supported languages: https://support.google.com/adsense/answer/9727
2. If your content is in a supported language, ensure it makes up the majority of text
3. Consider translating key pages if your primary language isn't supported

---

## 8. Duplicate AdSense Account

**What Google's email says:**
> "You already have an existing AdSense account."

**What it means:**
An AdSense account already exists with your information.

**How to fix:**
1. Only one AdSense account is allowed per person
2. Close the duplicate account
3. If you don't remember the old account, contact AdSense support

---

## Meta-Issue: Repeated Rejections

**Problem:** Each rejection and resubmission without substantial changes can make future approvals harder. Google may throttle review frequency for sites that keep resubmitting without fixing issues.

**How to handle:**
1. After a rejection, make SUBSTANTIAL changes — not minor tweaks
2. Wait at least 2-4 weeks between resubmissions
3. Document all changes made since last rejection
4. Don't resubmit until you've addressed every point in the rejection notice
5. Consider having someone else review your site objectively before resubmitting

---

## Pre-Submission Checklist

Before submitting or resubmitting to AdSense, verify:

- [ ] Site has 15+ pages of substantial (300-500+ word) original content
- [ ] Content demonstrates expertise and provides genuine value
- [ ] Clear niche/topic focus throughout the site
- [ ] No placeholder, "coming soon," or under-construction pages
- [ ] Clear, working navigation on every page
- [ ] About page with real identity/credentials
- [ ] Contact page with real contact method
- [ ] Privacy policy that mentions third-party ads, cookies, and Google
- [ ] Terms of service page
- [ ] ads.txt file at domain root with correct publisher ID
- [ ] HTTPS enabled with valid certificate
- [ ] Mobile-responsive design
- [ ] No broken links
- [ ] No prohibited content
- [ ] No copied or scraped content
- [ ] Content updated within last 30 days
- [ ] Site is at least 3+ months old (newer sites face more scrutiny)
