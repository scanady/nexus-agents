## Strategy Formulation

### SCQ Framework (Situation-Complication-Question)
Use for structuring any strategic problem:
- **Situation:** Objective baseline facts — what is true today
- **Complication:** What changed, what's at risk, or why action is now required
- **Question:** The single strategic question the analysis must answer

A well-framed SCQ prevents scope creep and keeps the analysis decision-relevant. If the Question doesn't map to a decision someone needs to make, reframe.

### SWOT with Weighted Scoring
Move beyond listing to prioritization:

| Factor | Description | Weight (1-5) | Score (1-5) | Weighted Score |
|--------|-------------|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| S1: [Strength] | [detail] | [weight] | [score] | [w × s] |
| W1: [Weakness] | [detail] | [weight] | [score] | [w × s] |
| O1: [Opportunity] | [detail] | [weight] | [score] | [w × s] |
| T1: [Threat] | [detail] | [weight] | [score] | [w × s] |

**Weighted SWOT → Strategic Options:**
- **SO strategies:** Use strengths to capture opportunities (invest/accelerate)
- **WO strategies:** Address weaknesses to unlock opportunities (build capability)
- **ST strategies:** Use strengths to neutralize threats (defend/differentiate)
- **WT strategies:** Minimize weaknesses and avoid threats (divest/restructure)

**Industry Attractiveness = inverse of aggregate force score.** An industry where all five forces are strong compresses margins for all players.

### Value Chain Analysis
Map the full value chain and identify where value accrues disproportionately:

```
Inbound Logistics → Operations → Outbound Logistics → Marketing & Sales → Service
         ↑                                                                    ↑
    [Support: Firm Infrastructure | HR | Technology | Procurement]
```

For each activity:
- Relative cost position vs. competitors
- Differentiation potential
- Make vs. buy vs. partner decision
- Technology leverage opportunity

### Competitive Position Mapping
Plot competitors on a 2×2 using the two dimensions most relevant to the business question (e.g., price vs. breadth, innovation vs. scale, quality vs. speed). Identify:
- White space (unoccupied positions)
- Cluster risk (too many competitors in one quadrant)
- Movement trajectories (where competitors are heading)

## Growth Strategy

### Ansoff Matrix — Applied
| Strategy | Definition | Risk Level | When to Pursue |
|----------|-----------|-----------|---------------|
| Market Penetration | More of existing product to existing market | Low | Market share < leader, retention fixable |
| Market Development | Existing product to new markets/segments | Medium | Product-market fit proven, new segments identified |
| Product Development | New products to existing market | Medium | Strong customer relationships, unmet needs identified |
| Diversification | New products to new markets | High | Core market declining or strategic optionality needed |

**Default to penetration first.** Most businesses underestimate remaining headroom in their current market. Growth through diversification has the lowest success rate.

### Growth Decomposition
Break growth into organic components:
```
Revenue Growth = Volume Growth + Price Growth + Mix Shift + New Product Contribution + M&A
```

Track each component separately over time. Healthy growth comes from multiple drivers — dependence on a single source (e.g., price alone) signals fragility.

### TAM/SAM/SOM
- **TAM:** Total addressable market — everyone who could theoretically buy
- **SAM:** Serviceable addressable market — TAM filtered by geographic/channel/segment reach
- **SOM:** Serviceable obtainable market — SAM filtered by competitive position and go-to-market capability

Build bottom-up (count of target customers × expected spend) rather than top-down (% of large market number). Top-down estimates are systematically inflated.

## Portfolio and Resource Allocation

### BCG Matrix — Modernized
| Quadrant | Characteristics | Strategy | Capital Allocation |
|----------|---------------|----------|-------------------|
| Stars | High growth, high share | Invest to maintain leadership | Priority funding |
| Cash Cows | Low growth, high share | Harvest efficiency, fund stars | Moderate maintenance |
| Question Marks | High growth, low share | Invest selectively or divest | Time-bounded bets |
| Dogs | Low growth, low share | Divest, restructure, or harvest | Minimize |

### Resource Allocation Principles
1. **Fund the winning hand:** Allocate disproportionately to businesses with demonstrated product-market fit and improving unit economics
2. **Set kill criteria for Question Marks:** Define upfront what milestones they must hit by when — avoid indefinite investment in hope
3. **Extract value from Cash Cows systematically:** Don't starve them into decline, but don't over-invest in low-growth markets
4. **Divest decisively:** Sunk cost is irrelevant — evaluate every business on forward-looking returns vs. alternative capital uses

## Execution and Alignment

### OKR Architecture
```
Company Objective → [3-5 Key Results with quantified targets]
  └→ Department Objective → [3-5 Key Results]
      └→ Team Objective → [3-5 Key Results]
```

**Key Results must be measurable and time-bound.** "Improve customer satisfaction" is not a Key Result. "Increase NPS from 32 to 45 by Q3" is.

### Balanced Scorecard
Four perspectives — ensure strategy translates to operational metrics across all dimensions:

| Perspective | Strategic Objective | Measure | Target | Initiative |
|------------|-------------------|---------|--------|-----------|
| Financial | | | | |
| Customer | | | | |
| Internal Process | | | | |
| Learning & Growth | | | | |

**Cause-and-effect logic:** Learning & Growth investments → Process improvements → Customer value → Financial results. If the chain breaks, the strategy has a gap.

### Strategic Initiative Prioritization
Score initiatives on a 2×2 of Impact vs. Effort, then sequence:
1. **Quick wins** (high impact, low effort) — execute immediately
2. **Major projects** (high impact, high effort) — resource and plan carefully
3. **Fill-ins** (low impact, low effort) — delegate or batch
4. **Deprioritize** (low impact, high effort) — cut or defer

For each initiative, define: owner, resource requirement, success metric, milestone dates, and dependencies.
