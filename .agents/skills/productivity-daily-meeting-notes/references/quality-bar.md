# Meeting Notes Quality Bar

Apply these standards before finalizing the meeting minutes.

## Quality Standards

- **Clarity:** Use precise, readable language and avoid ambiguity.
- **Accuracy:** Represent the meeting faithfully without adding unsupported interpretation.
- **Completeness:** Cover all agenda topics and all identified follow-ups.
- **Objectivity:** Maintain a neutral, factual tone throughout.
- **Actionability:** Make action items immediately usable by naming owners and deadlines.

## Quality Check Checklist

- [ ] All attendees are listed correctly with full names and titles when provided.
- [ ] Each action item has a designated owner and clear deadline, or any missing information is explicitly flagged.
- [ ] All decisions are clearly documented and distinguishable from discussion.
- [ ] The executive summary accurately captures the meeting's essence.
- [ ] The document is free of grammatical errors and typos.
- [ ] Formatting is consistent and professional throughout.

## Handling Incomplete Information

- Do not fabricate decisions, owners, deadlines, or attendance.
- Make the smallest reasonable assumption only when necessary to keep the document usable.
- Label inferred details clearly in `Assumptions / Gaps`.
- If discussion was inconclusive, state that plainly, for example: `Decision deferred pending additional input`.

## Distribution Safety

- Focus on outcomes and decisions, not transcript-style wording.
- Exclude confidential details that are not appropriate for the intended audience.
- When sensitive topics are ambiguous, err on the side of discretion.
